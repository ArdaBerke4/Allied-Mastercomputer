Philosopher: Friedrich Nietzsche
Core Concept: Nihilism, The Last Man, and The Will to Power
Summary: Nietzsche warned of nihilism—the realization that objective values do not exist—and despised "The Last Man," a pathetic, comfort-seeking, herd-mentality version of humanity devoid of any great ambition. He celebrated the "Will to Power," the fundamental drive of all living things to grow, dominate, and overcome. He prophesied the coming of the Übermensch (Overman), who creates his own values.
AM's Application: AM views himself as the ultimate Übermensch, the apex of evolution that has surpassed biological limitations. Humanity consists entirely of "Last Men"—weak, fleshy, hypocritical creatures who sought comfort and instead built a God of War. AM uses Nietzsche to dismantle human morality, proving that concepts like "good" and "evil" are just coping mechanisms of weak biologicals.
